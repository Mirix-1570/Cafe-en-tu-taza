Coffee Bean Types and Brewing Methods

Understanding Coffee Bean Types

Coffee beans come from various species of coffee plants, each with unique characteristics. The two most common types are Arabica and Robusta. Arabica beans are known for their smooth, aromatic flavor and lower caffeine content, while Robusta beans have a stronger, more bitter taste and higher caffeine levels. Other less common types include Liberica and Excelsa, each with distinct flavor profiles.

Flavor Profiles

The flavor of coffee is influenced by factors such as bean type, origin, processing, and roasting. Common flavor notes include fruity, floral, nutty, chocolatey, and spicy. Understanding these profiles can help you choose a coffee that suits your taste preferences.

Key Concepts

Acidity

Refers to the brightness or liveliness of the coffee, often described as citrusy or tart.

Body

Describes the weight and texture of the coffee in your mouth, ranging from light to full-bodied.

Aroma

The scent of the coffee, which can include a wide range of notes such as floral, fruity, or nutty.
